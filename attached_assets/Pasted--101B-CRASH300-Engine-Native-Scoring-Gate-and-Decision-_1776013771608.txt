## CRASH300 scoring framework to build

### Primary CRASH300 setup
BUY after crash-spike-cluster / swing-low exhaustion / reversal

### Secondary CRASH300 setup
SELL after extended rally / exhaustion cascade from swing high

The scoring model must be CRASH300-specific and structured around the actual CRASH300 behavior.

Implement a **6-component CRASH300-native scoring model**.

### Required CRASH300-native components

#### BUY side must primarily reflect:

1. Crash Spike Cluster Pressure
- measures crash-spike intensity and recency into the low
- this is the CRASH-specific version of spike pressure
- should reflect cluster behavior around lows

2. Downside Displacement
- measures how structurally stretched price is into the lower zone
- distance from recent lows / lower range context / lower structural placement

3. Exhaustion / Reversal Evidence
- measures whether the downward push is exhausting
- evidence that reversal conditions are starting to appear after the crash cluster

4. Recovery Quality
- measures quality of upward rebound / recovery after the low
- is price starting to reclaim properly rather than just bouncing weakly

5. Entry Efficiency
- measures how close the entry is to the swing low / optimal reversal zone
- avoid very late entries after too much of the rebound already happened

6. Expected Move Sufficiency
- measures whether the remaining upside runway is large enough for a true swing trade
- avoid high score where expected remaining move is too small

#### SELL side must be implemented as the correct CRASH300 secondary mirror:
- rally extension
- swing-high / elevated structure
- crash-spike persistence despite recovery
- recovery exhaustion
- downside cascade potential
- entry quality near elevated structure

Do not fake SELL logic using generic opposite-direction placeholders.

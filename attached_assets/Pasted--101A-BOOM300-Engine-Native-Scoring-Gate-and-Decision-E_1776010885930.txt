## BOOM300 scoring framework to build

### Primary BOOM300 setup
SELL after swing-high / spike-cluster exhaustion

The scoring model should be BOOM300-specific and built around components like:

1. Spike Cluster Pressure
- how many boom spikes recently occurred
- relevant windows such as short / medium / recent cluster context
- does the move look spike-driven rather than ordinary drift

2. Upside Displacement / Swing-High Stretch
- how extended price is into a local swing high
- distance from recent structural highs / upper range context
- is the symbol elevated enough to justify a SELL setup

3. Exhaustion Evidence
- signs the upside move is weakening after spike support
- failed continuation / reduced follow-through / weakening recovery behavior
- the rally is tiring rather than strengthening

4. Drift Resumption Quality
- evidence that the normal BOOM downward drift is reasserting itself
- the move is transitioning from spike-supported upside into resumed downside

5. Entry Efficiency
- how good the entry location is relative to the swing high
- avoid late entries after too much of the collapse already happened

6. Expected Move Sufficiency
- whether the structure still supports a worthwhile long-hold downside capture
- avoid high score on setups with weak remaining downside opportunity

You may refine the exact names, but the scoring must clearly be BOOM300-native and derived from BOOM300 behavior.
